How many people are homeless in 2020?

More than 58,000 people are homeless in NYC alone.

More than 19,000 of them are children.

How many of homeless people are single?

There are over 19,000 single homeless adults.

Over 4,000 being women and over 14,000 being men.

What causes homelessness?

One common problem that causes homelessness in New York is lack of affordable housing.

In the past 10 years, the amount of homeless people coming into shelters is now 52% higher.

Would social programs help end homelessness?

According to the U.S. Census Bureau Social Security had an estimated impact on poverty rate in 2015 by -8.34%. Similar social programs such as SNAP had a -1.44%. Refundable tax credits had a -2.88%. Housing subsidies, however, only has a -0.80% impact. Unemployment Insurance has a -0.20% impact.

Do parents who are homeless create a cycle for their children?

According to endhomelessness.org children who experience homelessness are shown to repeat a grade, be expelled, drop out, and have lower academic performance. In short, if someone experiences homelessness, they are more likely, to have their kids experience homelessness and the cycle continues.

Does education level contribute to homelessness?

According to knowledgeworks.org, a lack of a high school diploma or GED is one of the top factors for young adult homelessness. According to the website, they are 4.5 times more likely to experience homelessness. As well, children experiencing homeless are 87% more likely to drop out, which in turn brings us back to the previous question, that it indeed does make a cycle.

Should the government provide services to the homeless?

According to hhs.gov, there are already services for people who are experiencing homelessness. Some of which are safeline and hotlines for one to call in the cases of runaways or the victims of domestic violence, which are one of the main reasons for homelessness.
