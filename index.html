How many people are homeless in 2020?

More than 58,000 people are homeless in NYC alone.

More than 19,000 of them are children.

How many of homeless people are single?

There are over 19,000 single homeless adults.

Over 4,000 being women and over 14,000 being men.

What causes homelessness?

One common problem that causes homelessness in New York is lack of affordable housing.

In the past 10 years, the amount of homeless people coming into shelters is now 52% higher.

In NYC alone there are over 58,000 people who are homeless

In NYC many who are homeless happen to be people of color. What seems to be agreed upon is that one of the main reasons for homelessness is due to lack of affordable housing. Over 19,000 are apart of families and over 19,000 are children.

With so many people homeless, Dasani as well as Karol was apart of these statistics.

According to the Coaltition For The Homeless, at least 90% of the homeless are minorities. It is clear that minorities are disproportionately affected by homelessness. In recent years, the homeless population has increased and reached it's highest level since 1930's

Over the course of the 2019 fiscal year there has been about 132,660 different homeless people. According to the same study, this number also includes 44,300 children.

The main causes of homelessness is not only lack of affordable housing. Some of the main factors are in fact eviction, overcrowded housing, domestic violence, job loss, and hazardous housing conditions.

Studies show that, a majority of the street homeless have a mental illness or live with some sort of severe health problem.

According to the U.S Census Bureau the housing affordability gap has grown wider. Apartment rents have increased by 25% from 2005-2010 and rose every year following.

The rise in NYC homelessness are due to two major factors.

  1. NYC's worsening housing affordability gap!(Made even worse by the effects of the 2008 recession)
  2. The elimination of all permanent housing assistance for homeless families.

Solutions to homelessness

  1. Federal Housing Assistance:

    2. The most successful way to reduce homelessness because of the varity and uniqe options it provides. Housing Choice Vouchers and section 8 vouchers; these are vouchers that provide low-income families modest market-rating housing. This provides a a flexable subsidy that adjust with the family's income over time. This ensures that many families reamin stable in a home other than the shelter.

  2. Permanent Supportive Housing:

    3. This model combines affordable housing with suppot service and individuals living with mental illness and health conditions. Reasearch suggest that permanent supportive housing costs less than other forms of emergency and institutional care. New York Agreement is well known supportive housing initiative that successfully reduced homelessness in New York City and saved taxpayer dollars that would otherwise have been spent on costly shelters and hospitalizations.

  3. "Housing First":

    4. The Housing First approach includes moving homeless individuals directly into subsidized housing and then linking them to support services, either on-site or in the community. This gives a stable housing system in the long term for homeless individuals. This approach is far less costly than emergency and institutional care,such as shelters, hospitals and correctional facilities.
